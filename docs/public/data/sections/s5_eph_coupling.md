# Electron-Phonon Coupling

This section validates DFPT as a reliable source for the electron-phonon coupling $\lambda$ in simple metals, closing the second input channel of the ab initio workflow. The reasoning begins with the EFT expression for the electron-phonon vertex $g(k,q) = z^e \cdot \Gamma_3^e(k,q) \cdot g_0(k,q)$ (belief 0.86), derived from the microscopic $\lambda$ definition in Section III. A key approximation for the three-point vertex $\Gamma_3^e \approx m^*/m$ (belief 0.91) is established by induction from two independent lines of evidence: the exact Ward identity at zero momentum transfer (belief 1.00) and vDiagMC computations at finite momentum showing modest 10--20% corrections (belief 1.00). The composite strategy then shows that substituting $\Gamma_3^e \approx m^*/m$ into the EFT vertex causes the product $z^e \cdot \Gamma_3^e$ to collapse to unity via the Migdal relation, so the EFT vertex matches the DFPT Kohn-Sham expression (belief 0.85). Combined with the near-unity quasiparticle mass for simple metals (belief 0.86), which ensures the density of states also agrees, the conclusion is that $\lambda_{\mathrm{EFT}} \approx \lambda_{\mathrm{DFPT}}$ with few-percent corrections (belief 0.86). This two-step composite reasoning structure ensures that each physical assumption is separately testable.
