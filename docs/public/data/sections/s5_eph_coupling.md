# Electron-Phonon Coupling Validation

The second key ingredient for ab initio $T_c$ prediction is the electron-phonon coupling constant $\lambda$, standardly computed via density functional perturbation theory (DFPT). The paper validates this by deriving an effective field theory (EFT) expression for the electron-phonon vertex: $g(k,q) = z^e \cdot \Gamma_3^e(k,q) \cdot g_0(k,q)$, which factorizes the full coupling into quasiparticle renormalization, vertex corrections, and the bare matrix element (belief 0.86). An exact Ward identity at $q \to 0$ constrains the three-point vertex via $\Gamma_3^e = 1 - \partial\Sigma/\partial\epsilon_k = m^*/m$ (belief 1.00, prior 0.98), providing a rigorous anchor. At finite momentum transfer, vDiagMC computation of $\Gamma_3^e$ reveals modest 10--20% corrections that vary smoothly with $q$ (belief 1.00, prior 0.88). The crucial cancellation then follows: substituting $\Gamma_3^e \approx m^*/m$ into the EFT vertex and using the Migdal relation $z^e \approx m/m^*$, the product $z^e \cdot \Gamma_3^e \approx 1$, so the EFT vertex reduces to the DFPT Kohn-Sham expression. For this to extend from vertex-level to $\lambda$-level agreement, the quasiparticle density of states must match the band density of states, which requires $m^*/m \approx 1$ --- a condition satisfied to within 5--10% for simple metals at metallic densities (belief 0.86, prior 0.92). The combined reasoning chain drives the DFPT reliability conclusion from prior 0.50 to belief 0.86, with 0.00 bits of MI on the inference edge because the premises are already strong enough that the conclusion's shift is dominated by the posterior update rather than marginal premise information.
