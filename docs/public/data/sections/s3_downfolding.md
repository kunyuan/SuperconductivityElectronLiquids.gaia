# Downfolding the Bethe-Salpeter Equation

This section performs the central theoretical derivation: reducing the full momentum-frequency BSE to a tractable frequency-only integral equation. The pair propagator is exactly decomposed into a coherent BCS-like part and an incoherent remainder, and the cross-channel terms mixing Coulomb and phonon contributions are shown to be suppressed at $O(\omega_c^2/\omega_p^2)$ --- though this suppression claim (belief 0.69) is the weakest structural link in the derivation chain. The deduction from BSE kernel decomposition and cross-term suppression yields the downfolded BSE (belief 0.76), a one-dimensional integral equation with microscopically defined $\lambda$ and $\mu_{\omega_c}$ kernels. This framework is validated by a toy-model comparison: the full BSE gives $T_c/T_F = 10^{-5.668}$ while the downfolded version gives $10^{-5.667}$, a 0.2% agreement (both at belief ~0.76--1.00) that strongly supports the downfolding via abduction. From the downfolded BSE, microscopic definitions emerge for both $\lambda$ (belief 0.81) and $\mu$ (belief 0.84), and the BTS renormalization relation is derived as an exact corollary ($\mu$ scale independence, belief 0.99) rather than an ad hoc ansatz --- establishing an equivalence with the historically known Bogoliubov-Tolmachev-Shirkov relation. The Morel-Anderson constant-pseudopotential approximation is also justified microscopically (belief 0.92) from the energy-scale hierarchy $\omega_c \ll E_F$.
