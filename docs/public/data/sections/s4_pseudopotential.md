# Coulomb Pseudopotential from vDiagMC

Variational diagrammatic Monte Carlo (belief 0.83, prior 0.90) addresses this by stochastically sampling Feynman diagrams with bold-line (self-consistent) propagators, avoiding infrared divergences and enabling extrapolation to infinite order with controlled error bars. The homotopic expansion (belief 0.79, prior 0.88) dramatically accelerates convergence by continuously deforming the bare Coulomb interaction to incorporate partial screening at each perturbative order, reaching converged results at modest diagram orders ($n \lesssim 7$). Together these produce the highest-MI edge in the entire reasoning graph at 0.74 bits, feeding into the numerical $\mu_{E_F}(r_s)$ values. However, the conclusion itself stalls at belief 0.50 (prior 0.50) --- the noisy-AND inference model requires both premises to hold simultaneously, and their individual beliefs (0.83 and 0.79) cannot overcome the prior under this strict conjunction. The computed values are positive and monotonically increasing: $\mu_{E_F} \approx 0.21$ at $r_s = 2$ (aluminum-like) and $\mu_{E_F} \approx 0.33$ at $r_s = 3.3$ (lithium-like). These directly contradict the RPA prediction of attractive $\mu^*$, driving the contradiction node to belief 1.00 and confirming that RPA's prediction is an artifact of uncontrolled approximation.
