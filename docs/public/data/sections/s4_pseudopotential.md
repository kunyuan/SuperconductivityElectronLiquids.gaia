# Coulomb Pseudopotential

This section addresses the central computational challenge: evaluating the purely electronic four-point vertex of the uniform electron gas to obtain $\mu^*$ from first principles. The key tool is variational diagrammatic Monte Carlo (vDiagMC, belief 0.83), which provides a systematically improvable method for computing Feynman diagrams to high order with bold-line resummation and stochastic sampling. Convergence is dramatically accelerated by the homotopic expansion technique (belief 0.79), which reorganizes the diagrammatic series through a continuous deformation of the bare Coulomb interaction. Together, these yield the first controlled first-principles values of the Coulomb pseudopotential: $\mu_{E_F} \approx 0.21$ at $r_s = 2$ and $\mu_{E_F} \approx 0.33$ at $r_s = 3.3$, giving $\mu^* \approx 0.10$--$0.15$ after BTS renormalization (belief 0.50). The moderate belief reflects the noisy-AND conjunction of the method and expansion premises, plus a tension with the RPA prediction of attractive $\mu^*$: belief propagation through the contradiction factor suppresses the RPA claim to belief 0.25 while also drawing some probability from the vDiagMC result, reflecting genuine uncertainty about the quantitative reliability of these first computations.
